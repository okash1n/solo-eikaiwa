Last Saturday, I finally decided to clean out my closet. It had been a mess for months, and every time I opened the door, something would fall out onto the floor. I told myself I would just spend twenty minutes tidying it up, but of course, that plan didn't work out at all.

As soon as I started pulling things out, I found a box I hadn't opened in years. Inside were old photographs from my university days, concert tickets, and even a letter my best friend had written me before she moved abroad. I sat down right there on the floor and read through everything, completely forgetting about the cleaning I was supposed to be doing.

After a while, I made myself get back to work. I separated my clothes into three piles: things to keep, things to donate, and things to throw away. It turned out I owned about ten T-shirts I hadn't worn in at least two years, so those went straight into the donation bag. I also found a jacket with the price tag still on it, which made me laugh at myself for forgetting I even bought it.

By the time I finished, several hours had passed, and my back was a little sore from sitting on the floor for so long. Still, the closet looked completely different. I could actually see the shelves for the first time in ages, and it felt strange but nice to have so much empty space. I put the box of old memories back, but this time I placed it somewhere easier to reach, so I won't forget about it again.

That evening, I dropped off two bags of clothes at a donation center near my apartment. Walking home afterward, I felt lighter, almost the way the closet itself must have felt. I think I'll try to do this kind of cleaning more often, maybe once every few months, so it never turns into such a huge project again.
