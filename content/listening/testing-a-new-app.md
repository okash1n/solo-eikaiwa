I work for a small software company. My job is not to write code. My job is to test the app before other people use it. I look for problems. We call these problems bugs. Every day, I open the app on my phone or my computer and I try different things.

First, I click on every button. I want to see what happens next. Sometimes the app works well. Sometimes it does not work. For example, last week I pressed the save button, and the app stopped. The screen turned white. This is a bug. I write down what I did before the problem happened. This information helps the developers understand the bug.

After I find a bug, I write a short report. I take a picture of the screen and add some notes. Then I send the report to our team chat. The developers read my report and try to fix the problem. Sometimes they ask me questions like, what did you click before this happened, or does this happen every time. I answer their questions carefully because good information helps them fix the bug faster.

Every morning, our team has a short meeting. It is only fifteen minutes. We talk about new bugs and old bugs. We also talk about what we will test today. I like this meeting because I can hear about the whole project, not just my own work. It helps me understand how the app grows and changes every week.

I enjoy this job because I feel like a detective. I look for small clues and small mistakes that other people do not see. When I find a difficult bug, I feel very happy. And when the app finally works perfectly, I know that my work helped make it better for everyone.
