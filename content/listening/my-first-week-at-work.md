Last month, I started a new job at a small company in the city. On my first day, I felt very nervous. I did not know anyone, and the office was big and new to me. A woman named Sarah walked over and said hello. She was very friendly, and she showed me around the office. She showed me my desk, the kitchen, and the meeting room. After that, I felt much better.

Every morning, I arrive at the office at nine o'clock. First, I check my email and make a cup of coffee. Then I talk with my team about our work for the day. My manager is kind, and he always answers my questions. Sometimes the work is difficult, but my coworkers are always ready to help me. I am learning something new every single day, and I like that very much.

At twelve o'clock, we have lunch together. Most days, I eat lunch with Sarah and two other coworkers. We go to a small restaurant near the office, or sometimes we eat in the kitchen and talk. We talk about our weekend plans, our families, and our favorite shows. Lunch is my favorite part of the day because it is a good time to relax and talk with people.

On Friday afternoons, we have a team meeting. Everyone sits together in the meeting room, and we talk about the week. We share good news and talk about problems, too. My manager always says thank you to the team. After the meeting, some people go home, but other people stay a little longer and talk. I like this meeting because it helps me understand my job better.

Now, after one month, I feel much more comfortable at work. I know my coworkers' names, and I understand my daily tasks. The job is still a little busy, but it is also interesting. I am happy that I found this job, and I hope to learn many new things in the future.
