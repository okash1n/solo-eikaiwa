Let me tell you about my job. I work on a small app with three other people. We build tools that help stores count their money. It's not fancy, but I like it. Most days I'm just writing code and drinking too much coffee.

Yesterday something funny happened. A user sent us a message. She said a button didn't work on her phone. When she tapped it, nothing came up. That's annoying, right? So I said I'd take a look.

At first I couldn't find the problem. I read the code again and again. It all looked fine to me. Then my coworker walked by and pointed at one line. I'd made a tiny typo. Just one small letter was wrong. I'm glad he saw it, because I was getting tired.

So I fixed it and tested the button. This time it worked. I felt happy, like it was a small win. I sent the new code to the team. They checked it too, and it was good. Don't you love it when a bug turns out to be easy?

Later I wrote back to the user. I said thank you for telling us. She was really nice about it. That's the part I enjoy most. We're not just writing code all day. We're helping real people, and that feels good.
