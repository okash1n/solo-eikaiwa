You know what I love about being a developer? Honestly, it's the quiet moments while I wait for the build to finish. I push my code, and then the pipeline starts. I get a coffee. It's kind of nice.

But it's not always relaxing. Sometimes the build fails, and I have no idea why. Don't you hate it when the error message just says something broke, but it doesn't tell you where? I stare at the log for ten minutes. Then I find it. It's a tiny typo. Just one missing comma. So annoying.

This morning we had a fun one. Our test kept failing on the server, but it passed fine on my laptop. That's the worst kind of bug. My teammate and I looked at it together. It turned out the server was using a different time zone. We didn't even think about that at first.

I'm really glad we work as a team. When I'm stuck, I just ask someone in our chat. Usually another person has seen the same problem before. Plus it feels good to help others too. Last week I fixed a bug for a new coworker, and she was so happy.

So yeah, my job isn't only about writing code. A lot of it is waiting, checking logs, and talking to people. It can be slow, and sometimes it's stressful. But when everything finally turns green and the app works, that feeling is really great. That's why I keep doing it.
