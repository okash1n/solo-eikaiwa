title_ja: "締め切り前の静けさ"

Our team has a big deadline this Friday, and something strange happens every time we get close to one. The office gets really quiet. Nobody is chatting near the coffee machine, and even the phones seem to ring less often. At first, I thought everyone was just busy, but now I think it's more about focus. When people know exactly what they need to finish, they stop wasting energy on small talk.

This week, my manager asked me to check the numbers in our sales report before we send it to the client. It sounds like a simple job, but when I opened the file, I noticed a few things that didn't match the numbers from last month. Instead of guessing what was wrong, I went back to the original data and compared each line carefully. It took longer than I expected, but I found two mistakes that could have caused a real problem later.

After I fixed the report, I sent a short message to my manager explaining what I had changed and why. I didn't want her to be surprised if she noticed the numbers were different from the first version. She replied quickly and thanked me for catching the mistakes before the client saw them. That small moment made me feel like my work actually mattered, not just as a task to finish, but as something that protected the team's reputation.

By Thursday afternoon, the office started to feel different again. People began talking more, someone brought snacks to share, and a few colleagues even joked about how tired they looked. It was clear that the hardest part was over. We still had a few final checks to do, but everyone seemed confident that we would meet the deadline without any last-minute panic.

On Friday morning, we sent the final report to the client exactly on time. My manager gathered the team for a few minutes, not for a long meeting, but just to say thank you and to remind us that mistakes are normal as long as we catch them early. I left that day thinking about how much I had learned, not about spreadsheets or reports, but about staying calm and communicating clearly when things get stressful.
