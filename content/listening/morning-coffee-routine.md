Every morning, I wake up at six thirty. Before I do anything else, I go to the kitchen and make a cup of coffee. I don't talk to anyone. I don't check my phone. I just stand by the window and wait for the coffee machine to finish. It only takes about two minutes, but for me, this is the best part of the day.

When the coffee is ready, I sit at the small table near the window. I like to watch the street outside. Sometimes I see my neighbor walking her dog. Sometimes I see students walking to school with big bags on their backs. I drink my coffee slowly. I never drink it fast, even when I am busy. This quiet time helps me feel calm before a busy day.

On weekends, my routine is a little different. I still make coffee, but I make it for two people because my sister often comes to my apartment. We sit together and talk about our week. She tells me about her job, and I tell her about mine. We don't talk about big things. We just talk about small, easy things, like food or a new show we watched.

Some people think coffee is only a drink, but for me, it is more than that. It is a moment to stop and think. It gives me time before the noise of the day starts. I don't need a big breakfast or a long morning. I only need ten quiet minutes with my coffee, and then I feel ready for anything.
