Every time I finish a feature, I open what we call a pull request. This is basically a request for my teammates to look at my code before it becomes part of the main project. At first, this process felt a little scary. I remember worrying that someone would find a careless mistake and think less of me as a developer.

Last month, I submitted a pull request for a new login form. One of my senior colleagues left several comments, and honestly, my first reaction was to feel a bit defensive. But when I read through her notes carefully, I realized she wasn't criticizing me personally. She was pointing out a security issue I had completely missed, and she explained exactly why it mattered. After I fixed the problem, she approved the code with a short, friendly message. That small exchange taught me that good feedback is really about protecting the product, not judging the person who wrote it.

Now that I have more experience, I also review other people's code regularly. I try to remember how it felt to receive harsh comments early in my career, so I always start with something positive before mentioning problems. If I disagree with an approach, I ask a question instead of simply saying it's wrong. Something like, have you considered handling this error differently, works much better than telling someone their solution is bad. I have noticed that when reviews are written this way, people actually read them carefully instead of getting defensive.

Looking back, I think code review has changed the way our whole team works together. Bugs get caught before customers ever see them, and junior developers learn much faster because senior engineers explain their reasoning instead of just fixing things silently. It also means that no single person is fully responsible for a mistake, since at least two people looked at the code before it was released. These days, when I open a new pull request, I don't feel nervous anymore. I actually look forward to hearing what my teammates think, because their comments almost always make my work a little bit better.
